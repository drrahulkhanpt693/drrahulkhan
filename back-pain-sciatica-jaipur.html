Slip Disc vs Sciatica — What is the Difference & Best Treatment in Jaipur

Dr. Rahul Khan PT January 2025 10 min read Physiotherapist, Jaipur

As a physiotherapist in Jaipur, back pain and sciatica are among the most common conditions I treat — and also among the most misunderstood. Patients often come to me saying "I have sciatica" when they actually have a disc herniation, or vice versa. Understanding the difference matters because the treatment approach is different.

Slip Disc vs Sciatica — What Is the Difference?

This is one of the most common questions I get from back pain patients in Jaipur. The short answer: a slip disc is the cause, sciatica is the symptom.

Slip disc (more correctly called lumbar disc herniation) means that the soft inner material of a spinal disc has pushed outward through a tear in the outer ring. This disc material can press against a nearby nerve root in the spine.

Sciatica is the name for the pain, numbness, or tingling sensation that travels down the leg along the path of the sciatic nerve. It is a symptom — it can be caused by a slip disc, but also by other problems like piriformis syndrome, spinal stenosis, or sacroiliac joint dysfunction.

Feature Slip Disc (Disc Herniation) Sciatica
What it is A structural diagnosis — disc material pressing outward A symptom — pain/tingling running down the leg
Location of pain Usually low back, may or may not radiate to leg Buttock, back of thigh, calf, sometimes foot
Cause Disc degeneration, sudden loading, poor posture Disc herniation, piriformis, stenosis, SIJ
Confirmed by MRI scan Clinical tests (SLR, slump test) + history
