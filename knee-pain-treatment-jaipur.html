Why Does Knee Pain Happen? A Biomechanics Perspective

Most knee pain is not actually a "knee problem" — it is a biomechanics problem. As a biomechanist, I assess the entire lower limb to understand why the knee is being overloaded. Common root causes include:

  • Weak quadriceps and glutes — the knee absorbs more load than it should
  • Poor hip control — causes the knee to cave inward (valgus collapse)
  • Tight hip flexors and hamstrings — alter knee mechanics during walking
  • Flat feet or overpronation — rotates the tibia inward, stressing the knee
  • Abnormal gait patterns — built up over years of compensating

The exercises below address these root causes — not just the pain. This is why patients who come to me for knee pain treatment in Jaipur often recover faster than those who only use painkillers or hot packs.

5 Best Physiotherapy Exercises for Knee Pain — Jaipur

1

Straight Leg Raise (SLR)

Why it works: Strengthens the quadriceps (the most important muscle for knee stability) without putting any stress on the knee joint itself. This is the first exercise I give to all knee pain patients in Jaipur — including post-TKR and severe OA cases.

How to do it: Lie flat on your back. Bend one knee, keep the other leg straight. Tighten the thigh of the straight leg, then raise it to the height of the bent knee. Hold for 3 seconds, lower slowly. Repeat 15 times, 3 sets.

15 reps × 3 sets Daily Zero knee stress
2

Terminal Knee Extension (TKE)

Why it works: Targets the VMO (Vastus Medialis Oblique) — the inner quad muscle that is almost always weak in knee pain patients. A weak VMO is a major cause of patellofemoral pain and knee instability.

How to do it: Stand near a wall. Place a folded towel behind your knee to create a slight bend. Press the back of your knee into the towel, straightening the leg fully. Hold 3 seconds, release. 15 reps × 3 sets.
